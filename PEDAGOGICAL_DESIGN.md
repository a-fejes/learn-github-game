## ğŸ” Emphasis on Search-Based Discovery

### Why Search Over Direct Links?

#### Pedagogical Benefits
1. **Learning Repository Navigation**
   - Teaches the SKILL of finding things
   - Real developers spend time searching repositories
   - Builds knowledge of GitHub's interface

2. **Independent Problem-Solving**
   - Reduces cognitive dependency on instructions
   - Develops troubleshooting skills
   - Makes students more self-sufficient

3. **Realistic Workflow**
   - In real projects, no one hands you direct links
   - Contributors must learn to explore
   - Building professional habits early

4. **Confidence Building**
   - "I can find what I need"
   - Reduces intimidation with large repositories
   - Empowers student agency

### Implementation: Level 1

**OLD APPROACH** âŒ
```
"Open Issue #1 in GitHub" â†’ Direct link to /issues/1
Students follow the link, click, comment, done.
Skills learned: Clicking links.
Confidence gained: Minimal.
```

**NEW APPROACH** âœ…
```
"Search for 'Suggestion Box' in the Issues tab"
1. Student goes to /issues
2. Uses search box to find "Suggestion Box"
3. Reads the issue content
4. Understands what it's about
5. Decides to comment
6. Skills learned: Searching, reading, understanding, participating
7. Confidence gained: "I can find things!"
```

## ğŸŒ€ Parallax: Guiding the Journey

### What Parallax Does
Background elements move at DIFFERENT SPEEDS than foreground, creating:
- **Depth**: You understand you're in a 3D world
- **Focus**: Where things move fast is where your attention goes
- **Progress**: Moving forward feels like exploring

### Level-by-Level Parallax Design

#### Level 0: Horizontal Organization
- Papers move at different speeds as you scroll
- Organizing papers = understanding organization
- **Effect**: "Order emerging from chaos"

#### Level 1: Vertical Search
- Issues move slower than search particles
- Finding it = spotlight effect
- **Effect**: "Discovery happening before your eyes"

#### Level 3: Forest Scene (Forking)
- Trees at different depths
- Fork creates two paths
- Your clone appears on one side
- **Effect**: "Safe space created for experiments"

#### Level 4: Library (License)
- Books on shelves at different depths
- Spotlight is interactive (you control it)
- License glows when found
- **Effect**: "Finding treasure in a library"

#### Level 5: Isometric Stage (PR)
- Characters at different depths
- Stage rotates slightly (you're center)
- Celebration happens all around you
- **Effect**: "You're the hero of this story"
